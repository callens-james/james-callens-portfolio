# Preview: 04_AI_Prompt_Library.docx AI PROMPT LIBRARY Sustainable Operations & AI Enablement — Ready-to-use prompts for ChatGPT and Claude HOW TO USE THIS LIBRARY Each prompt is designed for a specific sustainability task. To use one: Find the prompt that matches your task using the category headings. Copy the full prompt text. Open ChatGPT or Claude in a new chat. Paste the prompt, then either attach your document (PDF/Excel) or paste your data below it. Replace any text in [BRACKETS] with your specific details. Review all AI output carefully before using in reports or sharing with leadership. ⚠ IMPORTANT: AI as a drafting tool, not an authority Always verify numbers, dates, and factual claims before using AI output in reports, emails to leadership, or external communications. Claude and ChatGPT can make mistakes. You are the expert — the AI is your assistant. DOCUMENT EXTRACTION Utility Bill Data Extractor WHEN TO USE: Use whenever you receive a PDF utility bill (electricity, gas, water) and need to pull numbers into your tracker. You are a sustainability data analyst. Extract the following from this utility bill: 1. Billing period: start date and end date 2. Total electricity/gas/water consumption (with units — kWh, MMBtu, m³, etc.) 3. Total cost in USD (or local currency) 4. Service address or location name 5. Utility provider name 6. Account number (last 4 digits only for privacy) Format your response as a clean table with two columns: Field and Value. If any field is not clearly stated, write "Not found — do not estimate." Do not calculate or infer values not explicitly in the document. 💡 TIP: Works best with one bill at a time. For batches, tell Claude: 'Process each PDF separately and number your responses.' ESG Questionnaire Scorer WHEN TO USE: Use when reviewing supplier ESG questionnaires, audits, or self-assessments. You are an ESG analyst at a global B2B SaaS company. Review the attached supplier ESG questionnaire and provide a structured assessment: SECTION 1 — SCORES (rate 1–5, where 5 is best practice): • Environmental: [score] — justification • Social: [score] — justification • Governance: [score] — justification • Overall ESG Maturity: [score] SECTION 2 — STRENGTHS (top 3 ESG practices demonstrated with evidence) SECTION 3 — GAPS & RISKS (top 3 material gaps or concerns with evidence) SECTION 4 — RECOMMENDED ACTIONS (2–3 specific next steps for engagement) Be specific. Cite evidence from the document for every claim. Flag if any section was not covered. 💡 TIP: If the questionnaire is long, ask Claude to 'focus on the environmental and governance sections first.' Contract / Policy Key Terms Extractor WHEN TO USE: Use when reviewing vendor contracts, NDAs, or sustainability policy documents for key terms. Review this [DOCUMENT TYPE — contract/policy/agreement] and extract the following: 1. Parties involved (names and roles) 2. Effective date and expiration/renewal date 3. Key obligations for each party — list as bullet points 4. Any sustainability, ESG, or environmental commitments stated 5. Reporting requirements (what must be reported, to whom, how often) 6. Penalties, consequences, or exit clauses 7. Any unusual or non-standard terms worth flagging Format clearly with headers. Summarize in plain language — avoid restating legal jargon verbatim. At the end, list any questions I should ask legal or procurement before signing. 💡 TIP: Add 'Our company is ' so Claude understands which party is 'us.' DATA ANALYSIS & CALCULATION Scope 3 Quick Estimator WHEN TO USE: Use when you need a rough carbon estimate for a business activity and don't have exact data. I need to estimate the carbon footprint of the following activity: Activity: [DESCRIBE IN DETAIL — e.g. "A team of 6 flying round-trip from Indianapolis to London for a 3-day conference, plus 4 nights hotel each"] Using standard GHG Protocol Scope 3 emission factors: 1. Calculate estimated tCO2e for this activity 2. Show the emission factors used and cite their source (EPA, DEFRA, IEA, etc.) 3. Show your calculation steps clearly 4. Compare this to a virtual alternative (video call) 5. Suggest how we could offset or reduce this footprint 6. Note any assumptions or caveats Use conservative (higher) estimates when uncertain. 💡 TIP: For travel data, export from Concur/Egencia and paste a summary. Don't upload confidential employee data. Data Anomaly Checker WHEN TO USE: Use before submitting data to leadership or for ESG reporting — catches errors early. You are a data quality analyst reviewing sustainability metrics for a corporate ESG report. Review the following data [PASTE YOUR DATA TABLE HERE] and identify: 1. Missing values: List every blank or zero that likely should have a value 2. Outliers: Flag values that are more than 2x higher or lower than neighboring periods 3. Unit inconsistencies: Check if units are mixed (e.g. kWh vs MWh, metric tons vs short tons) 4. Math errors: Check any totals or calculations that appear incorrect 5. Coverage gaps: Are any expected sites, months, or categories missing? 6. Overall data quality score: Rate 1–10 with explanation For each issue found, provide: location (row/column), what you found, and suggested correction. 💡 TIP: Paste your data as a table directly into the chat, or upload the Excel file. Year-over-Year Comparison Analyst WHEN TO USE: Use when you have two years of sustainability data and need to explain the changes to leadership. Compare the following sustainability data across two years and provide an executive-ready analysis. [PASTE YEAR 1 DATA] [PASTE YEAR 2 DATA] Provide: 1. A summary table showing each metric, both years, absolute change, and % change 2. The 3 biggest improvements with likely explanations 3. The 3 biggest regressions or areas of concern 4. Whether overall trajectory is on track for a net-zero 2040 goal 5. A 3-sentence executive summary suitable for a board update 6. 2–3 recommended talking points for explaining unfavorable changes Tone: professional, factual, and constructive. Do not spin negative results. 💡 TIP: Works well after you've compiled your quarterly data in the Pipeline tracker. REPORTING & WRITING Sustainability Report Section Writer WHEN TO USE: Use when drafting sections of the annual sustainability report or investor ESG disclosures. Write a professional [WORD COUNT — e.g. 300-word] section for 's [YEAR] Sustainability Report on the topic of [TOPIC — e.g. 'Scope 2 emissions reduction']. Use the following data: [PASTE YOUR METRICS AND KEY FACTS] Requirements: • Tone: transparent, factual, forward-looking — never greenwash • Audience: institutional investors, enterprise customers, and employees • Include: what we achieved, what challenges we faced, what we're doing next • Avoid: vague language like "committed to sustainability" without evidence • End with: one specific, time-bound commitment or target • Do NOT fabricate any data not provided above After writing the section, flag any claims that should be verified by legal or finance before publication. 💡 TIP: Always have a human review for accuracy before publishing. Research and compare sustainability performance for the following enterprise SaaS companies on the metric of [METRIC — e.g. Scope 2 emissions intensity per employee]: Companies to compare: , Salesforce, ServiceNow, Zendesk, RingCentral, Twilio, Five9 For each company, find: 1. Any risks or dependencies to consider before automating 5.
